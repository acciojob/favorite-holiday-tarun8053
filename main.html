Uttrakhand


Uttarakhand is one of India’s most popular tourist destinations, known for its natural beauty, spiritual significance, and adventure activities. Nestled in the Himalayas, the state offers a mix of religious pilgrimage sites, hill stations, wildlife, and trekking routes.

  1. Nainital – A beautiful hill station famous for its serene Naini Lake and pleasant weather.
  2. Mussoorie – Known as the Queen of Hills, offering stunning Himalayan views and the famous Kempty Falls.
  3. Haridwar – A sacred city on the banks of the Ganga River, renowned for the spiritual Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri.
  4. Kedarnath – A holy shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, surrounded by majestic snow-capped peaks.
  5. Jim Corbett National Park – India’s oldest national park, home to Bengal tigers and diverse wildlife.
  • Kafuli – A traditional green leafy curry made from spinach and fenugreek leaves.
  • Bhaang ki Chutney – A unique tangy chutney made from roasted hemp seeds and spices.
  • Aloo ke Gutke – Spicy fried potato dish popular in the Kumaon region.
  • Phanu – A protein-rich lentil curry prepared from mixed pulses.
  • Bal Mithai – A famous sweet delicacy made from roasted khoya coated with sugar balls.
